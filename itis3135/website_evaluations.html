Website Evaluations

Costco

Costco’s website is designed for straightforward navigation, product discovery, and accessibility. Its layout is clean and purpose-driven, allowing users to find and purchase items efficiently.

  • Contrast: Good contrast with readable black text on white backgrounds and bright product images that stand out.
  • Repetition: Consistent fonts, buttons, and layout styles throughout the site. Product pages follow uniform templates.
  • Alignment: All elements are well-aligned in grids. Navigation menus, product listings, and banners have strong visual alignment.
  • Proximity: Related items are grouped logically, making it easy to navigate and understand the content.

Yale School of Art

The Yale School of Art’s website breaks traditional design rules. While it may reflect an experimental aesthetic, its usability suffers due to poor layout and inconsistent styles.

  • Contrast: Poor contrast in many areas. Text overlaps images, and some links are difficult to read due to background clashes.
  • Repetition: Minimal repetition; fonts, colors, and navigation styles change frequently between pages.
  • Alignment: Elements appear randomly placed with little regard for visual alignment.
  • Proximity: Related information is often scattered or loosely grouped, making it difficult to follow.
